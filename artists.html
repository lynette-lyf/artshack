Doãn Tú Ly

Doãn (b. 1989 Da Nang, Vietnam) is a multi-hyphenate visual artist, who has worked across a diverse breadth of mediums, motives and scale ranging from text, illustration, collage, light, sculpture to large scale installations. Her work primarily deals with time, memory, identity and space. She studied studio art and journalism at Georgetown University and the UCL Slade School of Fine Art and works across a breadth of media ranging from collage, photography, illustration, light, and installations.

Song Ye-Jun

Song (b. 1975 Seoul, South Korea) conceives his painting as a medium of reaction to our surrounding existence. He uses existing everyday spaces as subjects in order to portray the ambience of our environment via a personal state of aesthetic identity. A process of combining both structure and atmosphere to address the idea of familiarity, his work aims to create a connection for viewers to experience the evidence of the transience existence.
